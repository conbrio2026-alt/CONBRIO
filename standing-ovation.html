Standing Ovation: Rohit Kshetri Delivers Historic Speech as First Nepali Jazz Pianist to Receive CONBRIO Award

In a landmark moment for global piano art and representation, Rohit Kshetri was honored with a standing ovation at the CONBRIO 2026 awards ceremony. His acceptance speech highlighted the deep cultural significance of his achievement and the transformative impact on South Asian jazz performance.

Historic Achievement in Context

Rohit Kshetri’s award marks the first time a Nepali artist has been recognized by CONBRIO as champion in the category that embraces both classical excellence and jazz innovation. The judges commended his artistic narrative, technical mastery, and evocative creative voice, which bridge tradition with modern expression.

As the first Nepali jazz pianist to receive this distinction, Rohit’s victory is a milestone for cultural inclusivity and the international piano community’s commitment to diverse stories of excellence.

Rohit Kshetri’s Acceptance Speech

On stage, Rohit said:

"It is with immense pride and humility that I receive this honor. Standing on this stage represents the collective effort of every teacher, mentor, family member, and fellow artist who believed that music could transcend borders and reshape expectations. For years, I rehearsed in small halls in Kathmandu, dreaming of stages like this. Today, as the first Nepali jazz pianist to receive the CONBRIO award, I feel the weight of that dream and the responsibility to elevate a story that has been historically underrepresented. To the young musicians listening now: let this moment be your invitation to pursue brilliance without apology. Excellence is built in the quiet hours of practice, in the choice to practice the imperfect with relentless intention, and in the courage to share your voice with the world. This award is not merely a personal milestone; it is a statement about inclusivity in the global arts community. It affirms that talent, discipline, and authenticity unlock opportunity, regardless of ZIP code, nationality, or heritage. I accept this honor on behalf of Nepal’s entire creative community, from the conservatories of Kathmandu to the workshops and living rooms where young artists first fall in love with sound. I promise to be an active steward of this platform. In the months ahead, I will work to create mentorship programs, performance opportunities, and cultural exchanges that connect our emerging artists with international peers. Together, we will ensure that music remains a bridge—one that is accessible, generous, and transformative."

A Forward-Looking Message

Rohit announced an initiative to mentor emerging pianists in Nepal, focusing on jazz fundamentals, performance resilience, and international collaboration. He affirmed that the award will be leveraged to expand opportunities for underrepresented urban and rural talent through master classes and global partnerships.

His statement concluded with a call to the global piano ecosystem to invest in equitable access, cultural exchange, and artistic mentorship for the next generation of creative leaders.
